Get Inspired

Wanna go but aren't sure what to wear? Check out our inspiration gallery for ideas.

For authenticity, check out the back of dear old dad's closet or your Aunt Sally's boudoir. If you can't do that, there are excellent thrift shops in the area where you can find some magical prom goodness (remember, Goodwill works via your good will, so high five for supporting charity). If you want a more traditional look, costume shops are the way to go. Prices are typically around the $50 mark and they usually have a wider selection of sizes and styles.

  • Goodwill
  • Harris Costume Shop
  • Magic Etc. Costume Rentals
  • Junker Vals (Down from TCU. Visit her site!)

Big Brothers Big Sisters

Still on the fence about attending? Know that proceeds from this event go toward supporting the Big Brothers Big Sisters Program of Greater Tarrant County.

BBBS focuses on pairing at-risk youth with one-on-one long term mentors.

The Facts:

  • Our Littles are 90% less likely to be incarcerated after being paired with a long-term mentor.
  • It costs approximately $1,300 to match a child with a mentor and service that match for one year.
  • Youth incarceration costs the state over $100,000 annually, per child. 100x the cost of being paired with a mentor through BBBS.
  • There are over 700 kids on our list, waiting for a mentor just like you.

Want to make a real difference to someone with real needs?
